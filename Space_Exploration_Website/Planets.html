Planets of the Solar System

Explore the eight major worlds orbiting our Sun.

Mercury Venus Earth Mars Jupiter Saturn Uranus Neptune

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun. Its surface is covered in craters and has extreme temperature fluctuations due to the lack of a thick atmosphere.

Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a dense, toxic atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide. Surface temperatures are hot enough to melt lead, making it the hottest planet in our system.

Earth

Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has liquid water, a protective atmosphere, and a magnetic field that shields the surface from harmful solar radiation.

Mars

Mars is a cold desert world with the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System. Scientists study Mars closely for signs of past water and potential future human exploration.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a thick atmosphere, visible cloud bands, and a powerful magnetic field. Its famous Great Red Spot is a giant storm.

Saturn

Saturn is known for its extensive ring system composed of ice and rock. It is a gas giant with a low density and dozens of moons, including the large, icy Titan.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant tilted on its side, causing extreme seasonal changes. Its atmosphere contains hydrogen, helium, and methane, which gives it a blue-green color.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and another ice giant. It has strong winds, dynamic storms, and a deep blue appearance due to methane in its atmosphere.
